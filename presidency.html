Early Presidency

May 5 1862: Visits Fort Monroe and participates in attack on Norfolk, Virginia

Early Presidency

May 20 1862: Approves the Homestead Act, which gives homesteads to settlers on government lands

Early Presidency

December 1 1862: His Annual Message is read before both houses of Congress

Mid Presidency

January 1 1863: He signs the Emancipation Proclamation, which frees slaves in certain Southern jurisdictions

Mid Presidency

February 25 1863: Approves the National Currency Act, creating a national banking system

Mid Presidency

July 13 1863: Responds to mob violence in New York City where draft riots rage for several days

Mid Presidency

October 3 1863: Issues proclamation for a national day of thanksgiving

October 31 1864: Approves admittance of Nevada into the Union

Late Presidency

March 4 1865: Inaugurated for a second term and gives his inaugural address

Late Presidency

April 15 1865: Gives his last public address to a crowd on the White House lawn
