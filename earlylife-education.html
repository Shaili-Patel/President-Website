Early Life & Education

February 12, 1809

Born in Hardin County, Kentucky, to Thomas Lincoln and Nancy Hanks Lincoln

October 5, 1818

His Mother Nancy died of milk sickness. Thomas Lincoln left Abraham Lincoln and his older sister alone in Indiana while he traveled to Kentucky to remarry.

January 20, 1828

Sarah Lincoln Grigsby, Abraham Lincoln's older sister, tragically died in childbirth on January 20, 1828, at the age of 20.

1832

Abraham Lincoln runs for the Illinois General Assembly and loses.

1834

Lincoln was elected as a state representative from Sangamon County, Illinois.

1846

Lincoln ran for Congress as a Whig candidate and won a term.

November 4, 1842

Abraham Lincoln married Mary Todd on November 4, 1842. She was from a prominent Kentucky family and was well-educated.

August 3, 1846

Elected to the United States House of Representatives.

1847-1849

Serves 2-year term in the House of Representatives.

February 1, 1850

Edward “Eddie” Lincoln, the almost four-year-old son of Mary and Abraham passes away after an illness.

November 2, 1858

In 1858, Abraham Lincoln lost the Illinois Senate race to Stephen A. Douglas, despite gaining national attention from their famous debates on slavery.

May 18, 1860

Nominated as a Republican candidate for president.

February 20, 1862

Abraham and Mary’s son, Willie, dies of sickness. He was 11 years old. The family is devastated.

January 1, 1863

Issues the Emancipation Proclamation, freeing slaves in Confederate states based upon his constitutional war powers.

June 8, 1864

The Republican Party national convention nominated Lincoln to run for a second presidential term.

January 31, 1865

Congress passes the Thirteenth Amendment to abolish, or end, slavery in the United States. It was sent to the states to ratify, or approve, the amendment.

April 14, 1865

Abraham Lincoln is shot while attending a play at Ford’s Theatre.

April 15, 1865

Abraham Lincoln dies, Vice President Andrew Johnson becomes President.
